The Farmer Timeline

Suppressions that sparked revolts

Quick Tip

Consider visiting the about page before looking at the timeline below. this will show you a more detailed description and allow you to view it in another format.



Sept.17,2020

The ruling government of India, BJP (Bharatiya Janata Party), passes three laws and two bills, both of which are anti farmer. These bills remove the MSP (Minimum Support Price), and remove madis, forcing farmers to sell to corporates.

Sept.25,2020

Farmer unions across India call for "Bharat Bandh" (A shutdown of the whole country to protest the laws). The nation wide strike took place on December 8th as a response to "state-sponsered anarchy" while essential services like emergency services continued, the rest of the country was under total lockdown.

Sept.25,2020

The same day as the general strike, farmers who were vexed that their respective state government wouldn't take action against the bills or support them in any way, they called for march towards Delhi, the capital of India.

Nov.27,2020

After clashes between farmers and police on the Delhi border, the government gave farmers temporary and peaceful access to a small protest site. But this eventually changed and protestors soon spilt out onto highways, streets and other public areas in Delhi.

Dec.1,2020

On December 1st, Bilkis Dadi, an activist that became famous when she participated in the CAA protests, was arrested when she was on her way to support the farmers. She was arrested on an absurd basis.

Nov.26 - Present

As farmers stay on the Delhi border, unwilling to budge, many of them are dying. These deaths are either caused by suiciude, satyagraha (a fasting technique used by Mahatma Gandhi), natural causes, etc. Currently 60 farmers have passed away.

Oct.14 - Jan.20,2021

10 rounds of talks have taken place between the central government and farmer leaders, in which the government has proposed different offers on the bills, but the farmers have stood adamant on their stand and will only back down when the bills are removed.

Jan.26,2021

On republic day, January 26th 2021, farmers protested on a given road while the republic day parade continued nearby. But soon, a few protestors seperated from the rest and started heading towards one of India's most iconic buildings, the red fort, where they were soon met with police and had clashes with them. After tear gas, and other chemicals, a few farmers were able to get on top of the red fort and plant their nishan sahib flag (a religious flag) on one of the domes.
